About

Design that works at the system level.

I operate across the full stack of design delivery. I think about the screen and the system behind it. I understand technology well enough to make better decisions with engineers, content well enough to build systems that stay maintained, and business well enough to know when the right answer is to simplify rather than add.

I am particularly experienced in regulated and high-complexity environments where the cost of a poor design decision is not just a bad user experience but a compliance risk, a lost client, or a safety failure. That context has made me a more rigorous, more precise, and more commercially aware designer.

Approach

How I work.

Frame before solve

The most expensive design mistake is solving the wrong problem elegantly. I invest in problem definition before screens exist.

System not screen

Individual screens are symptoms. I design the underlying patterns, components, and content systems that make quality consistent at scale.

Close to engineering

I understand what I am asking engineers to build. I stay embedded through delivery and treat technical pushback as design information.

Outcome over output

Deliverables are a means to an end. I measure success by what changed for users and the business, not by what was shipped.

AI as leverage

I use Claude, Figma, and vibe coding to close the gap between design intent and production reality. Automation where it reduces friction, judgment where it matters.

Honest trade-offs

Good design requires making explicit decisions about what to deprioritise. I document trade-offs so teams can make informed choices, not guesses.
